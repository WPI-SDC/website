About Us

The Satellite Development Club (SDC) at WPI is a student-run organization that facilitates development of students’ technical and professional skills through the investigation of space technologies.  In less than a year, this relatively new organization grew from a dwindling group of five members into a thriving band of 30 students competing in the NASA Cube Quest Centennial Challenge as well as developing technologies for CubeSats in Low Earth Orbit (LEO).

our goal

The goal of the SDC is to give students the opportunity to work with companies and practice collaborative problem solving within an interdisciplinary team. They gain experience communicating through technical writing, design reviews, public speaking, and a dynamic work environment.

 

The SDC accomplishes this through the research and development of small inexpensive satellites such as CubeSats, providing a platform for inexpensive experimentation in space and reduced development time.

 

The SDC is nearing completion of its time in the NASA Cube Quest Centennial Challenge, a competition to design a small satellite destined for deep space, and is looking ahead to its next mission. Starting in October 2016, the SDC will take the plans, designs, and lessons learned from the NASA challenge and apply them to a satellite designed for Low Earth Orbit (LEO). This satellite will be a CubeSat, a small, inexpensive, industry standard platform often used by universities and companies to quickly get their own technology into space. In LEO, the CubeSat will test a payload for Deep Space Ecology, a startup working on Mars habitation, and solar panels for Alta Devices, an electronics company.
